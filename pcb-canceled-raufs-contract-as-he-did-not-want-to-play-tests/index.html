PCB canceled Rauf’s contract as he did not want to play Tests

February 15, 2024 | 2:34 pm

The Pakistan Cricket Board (PCB) has canceled the central contract of fast bowler Haris Rauf as he did not want to play Tests in Australia. A press release said today that the decision was taken after a ‘thorough hearing process’ and ‘consideration of all concerned’ by the PCB committee. The PCB also said that he will not be allowed to play in any foreign league until June 30 this year.

Pakistan recently asked Rauf to play a Test in 2022 for the tour of Australia. However, although he initially agreed , he later withdrew himself from the tour , claimed the then new chief selector Wahab Riaz. Although at that time Rauf played the Big Bash League in Australia. Initially, he delayed giving the no-objection letter to play in the league , but later it was given to him by the PCB.

After Naseem Shah’s injury, Pakistan played with an inexperienced pace attack led by Shaheen Shah Afridi. Amer Jamals shined but they were washed up in the 3 Test series.

In the press release of Rauf’s contract cancellation, PCB said that Rauf did not want to join the Pakistan Test team for the tour of Australia. An inquiry committee was formed in this regard. After that it has been decided to cancel Rauf’s central contract from December 1, 2023.

It also said that Rauf was granted a personal hearing on January 30, ‘in accordance with the principles of justice’. But the PCB did not find anything satisfactory from his side. “PCB says, playing for Pakistan is the ultimate honor and opportunity for any player. Refusal to be a part of Test squad without medical report or justifiable reason is violation of central contract terms,” PCB said.

Today Rauf’s contract cancellation is the second big event in Pakistan cricket. Earlier, team director and acting head coach Mohammad Hafeez was ‘thankfully bid farewell’ by them.

Recently , Syed Mohsin Raja Naqvi came to PCB as the fifth chairman in 15 months .
